# Decision 16 — Event-routing conformance (consumer side): canonical-key subscription filters, kind/subject handler matching

> **External ordering (operator-sequenced, not built by any notify phase).**
> This Decision consumes the **revised eventplane API** — `consumer.Event{Kind,
> Subject}` + `Event.Key()` (the `Type` field is deleted),
> `eventplane/routing.Match`/`routing.Key`, and the kind/subject envelope +
> canonical-key SSE framing — specified in `eventplane/project/design/` (its
> D1–D4) and realized by **eventplane plan phases 01–04, which must be BUILT
> before this Decision's phase runs**. It equally assumes appkit (the chassis
> consumer engine behind `Spec.Consumers`, and the reflection tool's
> `subscribes` rendering) compiles against that revision — appkit's own
> conformance is appkit's spec, out of scope here. Finally, notify's **live**
> behavior depends on its upstreams emitting the agreed keys: crm's and
> prompts' producer conformance (crm kinds keep their entity-prefixed names
> with subject `/<contact-id>`; prompts kinds `run.succeeded`/`run.failed`
> with subject `/<prompt name>`, per prompts D25) must be built before the
> end-to-end chain is true on a running suite — notify's own tests append
> those keys locally and do not wait on the upstream builds, but the phase
> cannot *build* until eventplane + appkit land.

**Problem.** The suite's event addressing is revised
(`docs/event-routing-design.md`): the envelope's flat `type` becomes routing
fields `kind` + `subject`, the SSE `event:` line carries the canonical key
`source:kind<subject>`, and every selector matches whole keys with the one
`eventplane/routing` matcher. Hard cutover, no compatibility period, inside
the no-live-data migration window. notify is a pure **consumer** and must
conform what it matches and what it declares.

**What notify does today (ground truth, not vacuous).** notify consumes two
upstreams through `Spec.Consumers` (D11) and produces nothing — no
`Spec.Feed`, no `Spec.Events`, no `Producer` hook, and its embedded migration
set is exactly `001_schema_migrations.sql` + `002_feed_offset.sql` (no outbox
table). Its event selection is type-string matching in `internal/push`:

- the **crm** handler (`push.Handler`) matches each event against its declared
  in-edge with `sub.Match(ev.Type)` — `consumer.Subscription.Match`, stdlib
  `path.Match` over the flat type — where `push.Subscription()` declares
  `{Source: "crm", Filter: "contact.created"}`;
- the **prompts** handler (`push.PromptsHandler`) classifies with
  `switch ev.Type` over the constants `eventRunSucceeded`/`eventRunFailed`
  (`"run.succeeded"`/`"run.failed"`), and `push.PromptsSubscriptions()`
  declares those same two strings as Filters;
- both subscription lists flow through `Spec.Consumers` into the chassis
  reflection tool's `subscribes` surface (appkit renders
  `{source, filter, description}` per entry).

**Decision.** notify adopts the revised consumer surface with the smallest
change that keeps each handler's existing matching *mechanism*:

1. **Upstream keys (fixed external facts, restated for matching only).** After
   the producers' own conformance: crm emits `crm:contact.created/<contact-id>`
   (kind `contact.created`, subject `/` + the contact's id — crm's kinds keep
   their entity-prefixed names); prompts emits
   `prompts:run.succeeded/<prompt name>` and `prompts:run.failed/<prompt name>`
   (subject `""` for a nameless prompt — prompts D25's pinned derivation).

2. **crm: declared-filter matching moves to the canonical key**
   (`internal/push/push.go`). `push.Subscription()` declares
   `{Source: "crm", Filter: "crm:contact.created/**", Description: …}` — the
   whole-key doublestar glob (crm's subject is always `/`-rooted, so `/**`
   matches every contact id; `**` crossing segments is the pinned eventplane
   dialect). The handler keeps its anti-drift discipline — it matches the event
   against the *declared* subscription — but the match becomes
   `routing.Match(sub.Filter, ev.Key())`; a `(false, err)` result is treated as
   a non-match, mirroring today's `Subscription.Match` posture (the filter is a
   compile-time constant, so the error arm is defense in depth). The decode
   error keys off `ev.Kind`/`ev.ID` instead of the deleted `ev.Type`. Payload
   handling, async best-effort push, and `ErrSkip`-on-poison are untouched.

3. **prompts: kind-exact classification** (`internal/push/prompts.go`). The
   constants `eventRunSucceeded`/`eventRunFailed` keep their string values, now
   as **kinds**; the handler's `switch ev.Type` becomes `switch ev.Kind` (and
   the `run.failed` body check follows). Classification is deliberately
   **kind-exact and subject-agnostic**: notify pushes for *every* run outcome —
   named or nameless — exactly as today, and its behavior does not hinge on the
   unpinned "`x/**` matches the subjectless key `x`" glob edge.
   `push.PromptsSubscriptions()` declares Filters
   `"prompts:" + eventRunSucceeded + "/**"` and
   `"prompts:" + eventRunFailed + "/**"` (built from the same constants the
   handler switches on, preserving the shared-constant anti-drift), with
   Descriptions stating the push fires for the outcome of **any** prompt run,
   named or not.

4. **The reflection `subscribes` surface restates the new keys** by
   construction: appkit renders `{source, filter, description}` straight from
   `Spec.Consumers`, so the conformance *is* the new Filter/Description strings
   — no other declaration exists. `notify` stops calling
   `consumer.Subscription.Match` anywhere (that method's `path.Match`-over-type
   semantics are stale under the revision; whether eventplane revises or
   deletes it is eventplane's concern).

5. **No producer side, no migration.** notify appends nothing, so there is no
   outbox migration to convert; `consumer.SchemaSQL` is unchanged by the
   revision (eventplane D1), so `002_feed_offset.sql` and its byte-equality
   guard stand as committed. The embedded migration set remains exactly its
   current two files.

6. **Tests conform on their existing substrates.** The handler tests feed
   `consumer.Event{Kind, Subject, Payload}` values; the integration tests'
   producer side appends `outbox.Event{Kind, Subject, Payload}` through the
   revised outbox and the real `FeedHandler`; the mock-ntfy substrate and the
   Spec-level assertions (D11's ids) are unchanged in mechanism.

**Rejected.**
- **Keeping `Subscription.Match` with kind-only filters (`"contact.created"`)**
  — `path.Match`'s dialect diverges from the suite's one matcher, and a
  kind-only filter misdescribes the in-edge on the reflection surface, where
  every other selector in the suite is a whole-key glob.
- **Matching the prompts handler via `routing.Match` against its declared
  filters** — a nameless prompt's outcome key is subjectless
  (`prompts:run.failed`), and whether `prompts:run.failed/**` matches it is
  not pinned by eventplane's Verification; kind-exact classification keeps
  today's "push on both outcomes, any prompt" behavior independent of that
  edge. The crm handler has no such edge (its subject is never empty), so it
  keeps the declared-filter mechanism.
- **One broad `"**"` filter per upstream (prompts D24's consumer shape)** —
  honest for prompts (which routes *after* lookup) but a misdeclaration for
  notify, whose in-edges genuinely are specific kinds; reflection would
  advertise "everything" while the handlers push on almost nothing.
- **Engine-side filtering (a Filter on `consumer.Config`)** — rejected by
  eventplane D4: the engine delivers everything; selection is the handler's.
- **A `feed_offset`/outbox migration "just in case"** — there is no outbox
  table and the cursor schema is untouched by the revision; a migration would
  be pure ceremony.

**Verification** (real substrates throughout: handler claims run the real
`eventplane/routing` matcher against a mock ntfy `httptest` server — the
existing push-test substrate; the end-to-end claim runs the real revised
outbox + `FeedHandler` + `consumer.Run` chain the existing e2e test already
uses; the structural claim reads the embedded migration FS and the eventplane
constant).

- R-ZCGU-FG9L — `push.Subscription()` declares Source `crm` and Filter exactly
  `crm:contact.created/**`, and the crm handler matches via the real routing
  matcher against the event's canonical key: fed
  `consumer.Event{Kind: "contact.created", Subject: "/<some-ulid>", Payload:
  {"display_name": …}}` it issues exactly the pre-revision ntfy POST (Title
  `New contact`, body = display_name) to a mock ntfy server; fed kind
  `contact.updated` with the same subject shape it issues none.
- R-ZEWN-6ZQZ — `push.PromptsSubscriptions()` declares exactly two entries
  with Filters `prompts:run.succeeded/**` and `prompts:run.failed/**`, and the
  prompts handler classifies on `ev.Kind`: kind `run.succeeded` pushes Title
  `Run succeeded` (body = session_name), kind `run.failed` pushes Title
  `Run failed` (body = session_name + `: ` + error when error is non-empty),
  kind `run.cancelled` pushes nothing — and a **subjectless** `run.failed`
  event (`Subject: ""`, a nameless prompt) still pushes, proving
  classification is kind-exact, not subject-dependent.
- R-ZG4J-KRHO — end-to-end on the revised plane: an event appended through the
  revised outbox (`outbox.Event{Kind: "contact.created", Subject: "/<ulid>",
  Payload: …}`, source `crm`) and served by the real `FeedHandler` over an
  `httptest` connection is consumed by `consumer.Run` with notify's crm
  handler and lands the `New contact` push on the mock ntfy server — the
  kind/subject envelope, the canonical-key frame, the handler match, and the
  push observably chain.
- R-ZHCF-YJ8D — no producer side survives the revision: notify's embedded
  migration set contains exactly the two files `001_schema_migrations.sql` and
  `002_feed_offset.sql` (no outbox migration), and `002_feed_offset.sql`
  remains byte-identical to the revised eventplane's `consumer.SchemaSQL` —
  adding an outbox migration, or a cursor-DDL drift, fails this.
