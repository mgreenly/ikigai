# Decision 23 — The human scorecard is itemized (diff-style), not count-only

**Decision.** `Scorecard.WriteHuman` renders every subject and claim partition as its **actual items**, never collapsed to bare counts. Today's output prints `covered=2 missed=0 extra=8`; this Decision turns each per-case block into a diff showing every found, missed, and extra item as text.

This is a **pure rendering change** — every datum is already retained on `CaseResult` from D21: `Subjects SubjectScore` (the `Found`/`Missed`/`Hallucinated` `type/slug` ids) and `ClaimText []SubjectClaimResult` (per subject: `Covered []ClaimMatch{Gold,Predicted}`, `Missed []string` gold text, `Extra []string` predicted text), populated for matched, missed, and hallucinated subjects alike. No type/scoring/judge/data-model change; `WriteJSON` is untouched. Only `WriteHuman` and its helpers change.

## The diff form

Three markers carry the partition, applied uniformly to subjects and claims:

```
= matched / covered   (in both gold and prediction)
- missed              (in gold, absent from prediction)
+ extra / added       (in prediction, absent from gold)
```

A case block shows: the existing metric/count summary lines (retained), then the **subjects** partition (each found/missed/extra subject by `type/slug` under its marker), then the **claims** partition grouped by subject. For a matched subject, each covered claim shows **both** the gold and the predicted claim that covered it; each missed claim shows gold text; each extra claim shows predicted text. For a missed subject, every gold claim renders as missed; for a hallucinated subject, every predicted claim renders as extra. Illustrative shape (not a pinned byte layout):

```
- meridian-freshcrate-acquisition (hard)
  subjects  precision=0.750 recall=1.000  found=3 extra=1 missed=0
    = entity/meridian-foods
    = entity/freshcrate
    = entity/dana-okafor
    + event/meridian-freshcrate-acquisition
  claims    precision=0.640 recall=0.875  covered=7 extra=4 missed=1
    entity/meridian-foods
      = Meridian Foods was founded in 1998.
          ~ Meridian Foods is a cooperative founded in 1998.   (predicted)
      - Meridian Foods will keep the FreshCrate brand independent for at least two years.
      + Meridian Foods is a regional grocery cooperative.
    event/meridian-freshcrate-acquisition            [hallucinated subject]
      + Meridian acquired FreshCrate for $40 million in March 2025.
```

The `~ … (predicted)` sub-line under a covered `=` carries the predicted wording. Ordering is the existing stable subject-id sort (D21), so two runs over the same data diff cleanly. The diff is **additive**: the precision/recall, `found/extra/missed` (subjects), `covered/extra/missed` (claims) count lines and the aggregate + per-difficulty rollups all stay exactly where they are.

**Rejected.**
- **Count-only output (the status quo).** Names the extras as a number but hides their text, so the operator cannot judge whether an extra is a model error or a gold gap. Rejected; this Decision fixes it.
- **Showing only the gold line for a covered claim (drop the predicted wording).** Loses *how* a weaker model phrased a fact the judge accepted. Show both. Rejected.
- **Adding the subjects' human `Name`/title (threading it through `Score`).** `type/slug` is the public subject identity already retained; adding `Name` would widen `SubjectScore`/`Score` for cosmetic gain. Keep rendering-only. Rejected.
- **Restructuring `-json` to carry the diff too.** `WriteJSON` already serializes the full `CaseResult`. This is a human-output gap only. Rejected.

**Verification.**
- R-8KX2-4ASY — `WriteHuman` over a `CaseResult` emits each found, missed, and hallucinated subject's `type/slug` under a distinct partition marker (`=`/`-`/`+`), not merely the `found=/extra=/missed=` counts — a found, a missed, and a hallucinated subject each appear as their own labeled line.
- R-8M4Y-I2JN — For a **matched** subject, `WriteHuman` emits the **text** of each partition: every covered claim shows both its gold claim **and** the predicted claim that covered it, every missed claim shows the gold text, every extra claim shows the predicted text — proven by asserting the actual claim strings appear, not just their counts.
- R-8NCU-VUAC — For unmatched subjects, `WriteHuman` emits the per-subject claim text: a **missed** subject's every gold claim renders as missed text and a **hallucinated** subject's every predicted claim renders as extra text, each grouped under that subject.
- R-8OKR-9M11 — The diff is additive: `WriteHuman` still emits the per-case precision/recall and `found/extra/missed`, `covered/extra/missed` count lines and the aggregate + per-difficulty rollups (nothing removed), and `WriteJSON` output is unchanged and still round-trips into an equal `Scorecard`.
