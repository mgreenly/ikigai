# Decision 3 — Personal-access-token management moves to the profile page

**Decision.** The **personal access token** surface — the create form, the list of
the owner's active PATs, and per-PAT revoke — **moves from the landing/home page to
the profile page**. The PAT *capability* is unchanged; only its location and the
post-action redirect target change.

- **View.** The PAT create form and the `pat_block` partial (the owner's PAT rows)
  render on the **profile** page (`handleProfile` populates `PATs` for the signed-in
  owner via `a.pats.ListByOwner`, exactly as `handleIndex` does today). They are
  **removed** from the logged-in `/` (landing) template and `handleIndex` stops
  populating `PATs`.
- **Create.** `POST /pat` is unchanged in mechanism (same-origin + `requireSession`,
  mint, audit, render the show-once `pat_created` confirmation). The
  `pat_created` confirmation page links **back to `/profile`** (not `/`).
- **Revoke.** `POST /pat/{public_id}/revoke` keeps its same-origin + `requireSession`
  guards and its not-found-on-mismatch behavior, but on success it
  **redirects to `/profile`** (`http.StatusSeeOther`) — the re-rendered profile list
  reflects the change — instead of `/`.
- The `pat_block` partial markup itself is reused verbatim from the profile
  template (it is in the shared parse set). No PAT data model, store, or route
  signature changes.

**Rejected.**
- **Leaving PAT management on the landing too (duplicated)** — two places to manage
  the same secret is the conflation we are removing; the landing must be free of
  token administration (product Scope).
- **Changing the PAT routes' paths** (e.g. `/profile/pat`) — needless churn; the
  routes keep their paths, only their redirect target and the page that embeds the
  form move. Same-origin + session guards are unchanged.
- **Keeping the post-revoke redirect at `/`** — would bounce the owner off the
  settings page they were working on back to the home page; the action's result
  belongs on the page that hosts the action.

**Verification.**
- R-DB06-PATM — `GET /profile` with a valid session renders the PAT create form and
  the owner's PAT list (the `pat_block` partial), reflecting the owner's active PATs.
- R-DB07-PATR — On success, `POST /pat/{public_id}/revoke` redirects to `/profile`,
  and the `POST /pat` show-once confirmation links back to `/profile` (not `/`).
- R-DB08-PATX — `GET /` with a valid session (the landing/home page) contains **no**
  PAT-create form and **no** PAT rows.
