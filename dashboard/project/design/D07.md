# Decision 7 — Login composition: control-plane tagline plus a name-origin colophon (name → its two parts)

**Decision.** The login page (logged-out `GET /`, the `.signin-wall` branch of
`ui/html/index.html`) keeps its existing function and framing — wordmark, the
**"Your account's control plane"** heading, the **"Sign in to manage access
tokens, connected agents, and the box's MCP services."** line, and the **"Sign in
with Google"** CTA, all verbatim — and gains, **below the CTA**, a **name-origin
colophon** that explains the portmanteau **hierarchically**: **ikigenba** is the
name (the subject), and it is explained by its **two subcomponents**, *ikigai* and
*genba*, shown subordinate beneath it.

This structure is the decision. The earlier cuts failed because they presented
**three peer statements** (a synthesis line, then ikigai, then genba) — flat, with
no parent. It is not three equal things; it is **one name and its two parts**:

1. **The name leads, with its meaning (the parent).** A single lede line states
   the word and what it means, and labels it a portmanteau:

   > **ikigenba** — where your livelihood actually gets done. A portmanteau of two
   > Japanese words:

2. **Its two subcomponents sit beneath it, subordinate (the children).** A
   two-item list, visually indented under the lede (a left hairline rule marks the
   parent→parts relationship), gives each source word by romaji + kanji and one
   clause of meaning. In each, the **fragment that survives into "ikigenba" is
   highlighted** — full-ink against the otherwise-subtle word — so reading the
   highlights top-to-bottom spells the name: **iki** · **genba**.
   - **iki**gai · 生き甲斐 — "reason for being," the work worth doing; your business.
   - **genba** · 現場 — "the actual place," the floor where the work really happens.

The highlighted fragments are how the splice shows without a third peer line: the
two parts *are* the explanation, and they visibly compose the name above them.

**Diminished, but not flat.** "Diminished" governs *prominence* — it must never
rival the blue sign-in CTA: no accent color, no fill, no icon, set off by a single
hairline rule, muted/subtle ink. It does **not** mean flat: the name→parts
hierarchy and the highlighted fragments give it real structure in neutral grays.

- **Placement — below the CTA, last in `.signin-wall`.** The sign-in action stays
  the visual priority; the explanation sits underneath it like a colophon.

- **Markup.** Added to the `{{else}}` (logged-out) branch of `index.html`, after
  the existing sign-in `<a>`:

  ```html
  <aside class="name-origin" aria-label="What ikigenba means">
    <p class="name-origin-lede"><b>ikigenba</b> — where your livelihood actually gets done. A portmanteau of two Japanese words:</p>
    <dl class="name-origin-parts">
      <div>
        <dt><b class="seam">iki</b>gai <span lang="ja">生き甲斐</span></dt>
        <dd>&ldquo;reason for being&rdquo; — the work worth doing; your business.</dd>
      </div>
      <div>
        <dt><b class="seam">genba</b> <span lang="ja">現場</span></dt>
        <dd>&ldquo;the actual place&rdquo; — the floor where the work really happens.</dd>
      </div>
    </dl>
  </aside>
  ```

  Note the structure: **one** `<p>` (the name + meaning), then **one** `<dl>` of
  exactly **two** parts. There is no third sibling line — the two `dt/dd` pairs are
  children of the name, not peers of it.

- **Styling.** New rules appended to `ui/static/app.css` after the `.signin-wall`
  block, semantic tokens only (no hard-coded color/space/radius). The `<p>`
  override must out-specify `.signin-wall p` (`(0,1,1)`, which would otherwise
  force body-lg/centered) — hence the descendant selector `(0,2,0)`. The left rule
  + indent on `.name-origin-parts` is what makes the two parts read as subordinate
  to the name above them:

  ```css

  /* Explains the "ikigenba" portmanteau as a name (the lede) with its two
  ```

This decision governs only the **login** page's composition. It does not touch the
landing/home page (D5), the profile page (D2–D4), routing (D1), the `/services`
inventory endpoint, the install scripts, or any schema. It is a pure template +
CSS change confined to the `{{else}}` branch of `index.html` and one appended
`app.css` block.

**Rejected.**
- **Three peer lines (synthesis, then ikigai, then genba)** — the original flat
  cut. It reads as three equal statements with no parent; the name's meaning gets
  no more weight than a root gloss. Replaced by name-as-parent + two subordinate
  parts.
- **A left-to-right word-equation (`ikigai + genba = ikigenba`)** — it shows the
  splice but still reads as three coordinate tokens and puts the name last, as a
  result, rather than first, as the subject being explained. The hierarchy
  (name on top, parts beneath) is the correct frame.
- **Replacing the "control-plane" tagline with the name story** — the tagline tells
  a returning owner what the page *does*; the name story is secondary. Keep the
  tagline verbatim; add the story beneath it.
- **Putting the colophon between the tagline and the sign-in button** — demotes the
  primary action; the CTA stays directly under the tagline.
- **Showing the colophon on the logged-in landing/home too** — a signed-in owner is
  already oriented; the home page is for connecting and navigating (D5). The block
  lives only in the logged-out branch.
- **Coloring the name or the highlighted fragments with the accent** — that makes
  the block rival the blue CTA; the hierarchy is carried by ink weight and the
  indent rule, in neutral grays only.

**Verification.**
- R-DB17-ORIG — The logged-out `GET /` renders the name-origin colophon as **a name
  with two subcomponents, not three peers**: the body contains a single lede naming
  `ikigenba`, calling it a portmanteau, and giving its meaning (`where your
  livelihood actually gets done`); and a `name-origin-parts` list of **exactly two**
  items — `ikigai` (生き甲斐) and `genba` (現場) — each with its surviving fragment
  marked (`seam`) and a one-clause gloss. The two parts are structurally subordinate
  to the lede (the `name-origin-parts` list is distinct from the lede paragraph),
  and there is no third sibling explanation line.
- R-DB18-KEEP — The logged-out `GET /` still renders the control-plane framing
  verbatim: the heading `Your account's control plane`, the line `Sign in to manage
  access tokens, connected agents, and the box's MCP services.`, and the
  `Sign in with Google` CTA linking to `/login`.
- R-DB19-LAND — The name-origin colophon appears **only** on the logged-out login
  page: the signed-in `GET /` landing/home body omits the `name-origin` block.
